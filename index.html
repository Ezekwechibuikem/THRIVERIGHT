Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to some of the most common questions about sickle cell disease and our awareness efforts.

What is sickle cell disease?

Sickle cell disease (SCD) is a group of inherited red blood cell disorders. People with SCD have red blood cells that become hard and sticky and take on a C-shape, like a sickle. These cells block blood flow, causing pain, infections, and complications.

How can I know my genotype?

You can know your genotype through a simple blood test conducted at a certified laboratory or during one of our Thriveright outreach programs. Early genotype awareness is essential for making informed health and relationship decisions.

Can someone with sickle cell live a normal life?

Yes. With proper medical care, healthy lifestyle choices, and regular checkups, individuals with sickle cell disease can live fulfilling and productive lives. Support, awareness, and access to treatment greatly improve quality of life.

Is sickle cell disease contagious or caused by spiritual forces?

No. Sickle cell is a genetic condition inherited from both parents. It is not contagious and has no connection to spiritual or supernatural causes. Debunking such myths is part of our awareness mission.

What does Thriveright Health Foundation do to support sickle cell awareness?

We conduct school and community outreaches, offer free genotype testing, provide educational resources, and partner with health professionals to promote awareness, early detection, and healthy decision-making.
